## Detailed Analysis

### Revenue Concentration

* No product contributes more than 50% of revenue in any category.
* Category 14 is the most noticeable: the top 3 products contribute about 34.4% of total revenue, while the category has the lowest total revenue (~340k).

---

### Category Behavior

**Dependence on top products**

* Category 14 has the lowest revenue, and the top 1 product contributes more than 11%, so it is the most fragile category.
* Categories 3, 4, 5, and 9 have top products contributing more than 9%, which shows some level of concentration.

**Even distribution**

* Category 10 has a very small gap between top products (~0.3%), showing a nearly equal distribution.
* Category 2 has top 3 contributions between 5.7% and 6.2%, and it also has the highest total revenue.
* Category 12 has almost the same contribution for the top 3 products (difference of ~0.09%).

---

### Risk Detection

* If the top 1 product disappears, no category will collapse completely, but the risk level is different.
* Category 14 has the highest risk because it has low total revenue and higher concentration (top 1 ~12%, top 3 ~34%). Losing one of these products will have a clear impact.
* Categories 3, 5, and 9 have top products close to 10%, but their larger size helps reduce the risk.
* Categories 2, 10, and 12 have low risk because revenue is more evenly distributed. Losing one product will not affect the category too much.

---

### Business Implications

* For Category 14: focus on growing other products outside the top 3 to reduce dependence.
* For Categories 10 and 12: focus on overall category growth instead of pushing one product.
* Category 2: protect and scale as the main revenue driver.
* For Categories 4 and 5: test whether pushing the top product can increase category revenue.

---

### Conclusion

Overall, the portfolio of 15 categories is relatively healthy, with no extreme concentration.

However, Category 14 is a weak point due to low revenue and higher concentration, making it more sensitive to changes in top products.

Categories 10 and 12 show a stable and balanced structure.

The priority should be to stabilize Category 14 while protecting and scaling Category 2.
